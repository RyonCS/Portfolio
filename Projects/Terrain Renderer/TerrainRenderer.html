Interactive Terrain Simulation

Northeastern University, Spring 2024
OpenGL, C++

This is a final project for my Computer Graphics course and represents a culmination of the skills I learned during the course.

First, I generated a plane of vertices and applied a height map to adjust their y-values, creating varied terrain. Then, I formed quads from two triangles for every four vertices. Using multi-texturing in the fragment shader, I created a realistic gradient of water, grass, rock, and snow based on the y-values. Additionally, I implemented a skybox to surround the terrain and a rotating light to simulate the sun. Lastly, I used bilinear interpolation to attach the camera to the scene, simulating a person walking on the jagged terrain.
